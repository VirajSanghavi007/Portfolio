Finance · Algorithmic Trading Ongoing

AlgoTrade-X

A production-grade algorithmic trading platform that runs a 7-model ensemble (classical ML, deep learning, and reinforcement learning) over equities, ETFs, and crypto. Built with 100% free data sources and deploys end-to-end via Docker.

Python PyTorch XGBoost LightGBM HuggingFace FastAPI Streamlit Docker Redis

Problem

Retail algorithmic trading tools are either black boxes or academic toys. The goal was to build something with institutional-grade risk controls, real explainability, and a multi-model ensemble that adapts to different market regimes.

Architecture

Seven models form the ensemble: XGBoost and LightGBM (with Optuna tuning), Random Forest, LSTM with temporal attention (3 layers, sequence length 60), Temporal Convolutional Network (dilated causal, receptive field 765 bars), PatchTST Transformer, and a PPO reinforcement learning agent trained with curriculum learning. A Ridge meta-learner combines their outputs.

  • HMM regime detector (3 states: bull / bear / sideways) routes signals to per-regime ensemble weights
  • 80+ engineered features: momentum, volatility, volume, microstructure, sentiment (FinBERT on headlines), fundamentals
  • Walk-forward backtesting: 2-year training window, 3-month test window, no lookahead bias guaranteed by test suite
  • Data from free APIs: yfinance, Finnhub, FRED, Binance, NewsAPI, SEC EDGAR, Alpaca paper trading
  • SHAP explainability on every prediction. Every trade decision logged with full feature snapshot

Risk Controls

Seven circuit breakers (portfolio drawdown, daily loss, single position loss, VIX spike, volatility spike, correlation crisis, low model confidence). Position sizing uses half-Kelly scaled by volatility targeting and ensemble confidence.

Key Learnings

Walk-forward validation is non-negotiable. A strategy that looks good on a single train/test split almost always falls apart under WFO. Regime-aware weighting made a bigger difference than adding more models to the ensemble.

Speech AI · ASR Ongoing

ADAPT-Synthetix

A research-grade adaptive speech pipeline that goes beyond transcription. It diagnoses WHY ASR fails at the phoneme level, fingerprints acoustic noise, detects model drift, and closes the loop with targeted LoRA fine-tuning,all in a single system.

Python Wav2Vec2 PyTorch AdaLoRA Bark TTS FastAPI React PostgreSQL Docker

Problem

Standard ASR systems return a transcript and stop. They don't tell you which phonemes are causing errors, what the acoustic environment is doing to the signal, or how the model is drifting over time. ADAPT-Synthetix was designed to close all three gaps.

Architecture

Built on Facebook's Wav2Vec2 with a multi-layer diagnostic engine sitting on top. Every transcription runs through CTC confidence scoring, phoneme error rate computation, reference alignment, and noise fingerprinting before entering a confidence-weighted remediation priority queue.

  • 8-feature acoustic noise fingerprinting: spectral centroid, bandwidth, rolloff, ZCR, RMS, MFCC variance, tempo, harmonic ratio. Classifies into clean / traffic / crowd / machinery / indoor.
  • Phoneme-level drift monitoring using CUSUM and MMD. Triggers fine-tuning alert when 5+ phonemes enter high-risk state across a 20-utterance window.
  • Domain vocabulary injection for medical and emergency terms,these errors are escalated in the priority queue regardless of overall CER.
  • AdaLoRA (adaptive rank) fine-tuning with layer freezing and 10% experience replay. Implements HDMoLE LoRA-MoE routing across multiple expert adapters.
  • Dual frontend: vanilla JS terminal UI (mic, waveform, file upload) and a React 18 dashboard with analytics, phoneme explorer, and model hub pages.

Research Papers Implemented

Rumberg et al. Interspeech 2023 (CTC token uncertainty), Ernez et al. PMLR 2023 (conformal prediction priority queue), Zhang et al. ICLR 2023 (AdaLoRA), Mu et al. ICASSP 2025 (HDMoLE), Guo et al. ICML 2017 (ECE calibration).

Key Learnings

Phoneme-level granularity reveals failure modes that word-level CER completely masks. The drift detection was the hardest part: CUSUM is sensitive to window size in ways that are not obvious until you see it fail on real-world noisy audio.

Logistics · Supply Chain Ongoing

FreightMind

A healthcare supply chain analytics platform focused on global pharmaceutical logistics. Uses GHSC formulary data, EXIM trade records, and country-level trade volumes to surface patterns and optimization opportunities in the flow of essential medicines.

Python Pandas NumPy PDF Parsing Trade APIs

Problem

Global healthcare supply chains for essential medicines are opaque. Procurement decisions, trade policy changes, and logistical bottlenecks affect whether medicines reach patients. There is no unified analytical view across GHSC formulary data, EXIM trade regulations, and country-level import/export volumes.

Data Sources

  • GHSC (Global Health Supply Chain) Essential Medicines Formulary and HIV Rapid Diagnostic Tests catalogue
  • GHSC-PSM FY24 Annual Report covering procurement and supply chain performance
  • Foreign Trade (Development and Regulation) Act 1992 and 2010 amendment
  • Country-wise total trade volumes (EIDB dataset) and EXIM policy documents

Approach

Parsing and structuring unstructured PDF and Excel sources into a unified trade and formulary dataset. Planned ML components include demand forecasting for essential medicines and trade route optimization under regulatory constraints.

Finance · Fraud Detection iDEA 2.0

AML Intelligence Platform

Graph-native Anti-Money Laundering detection system built for Union Bank of India in 48 hours. Ingests 500K synthetic transactions, constructs a directed account graph, and classifies 8 AML typologies using structural graph analysis and a Random Forest classifier.

Python NetworkX scikit-learn FastAPI Cytoscape.js Chart.js

Problem

Union Bank's fraud investigators manually reconstruct transaction trails, taking 3 to 5 days per case. Coordinated laundering schemes (circular routing, layering, smurfing) escape detection before funds exit.

Detection Pipeline

  • 8 AML typologies: Cycle, Fan-Out, Fan-In, Scatter-Gather, Gather-Scatter, Bipartite, Stack, Random Chain
  • 7 behavioural signals: rapid fan-out, round-trip, structuring below $10K, layering velocity, dormant activation, currency mismatch, smurfing
  • Random Forest (200 trees, 16 graph features): F1 0.67, Precision 0.83, Accuracy 0.86

Logistics · Trade LOC Hackathon

Swipe to Export

ML model that matches exporters with relevant importers from structured trade datasets. Built under hackathon constraints using PCA dimensionality reduction and Bayesian probabilistic confidence scoring.

Python Scikit-learn PCA Bayesian Inference

Problem

Exporters struggle to find relevant international buyers in vast trade datasets. The hackathon challenge was to build a matching model from real structured trade data under time pressure.

Approach

  • PCA for dimensionality reduction on high-dimensional trade feature space
  • Bayesian probabilistic scoring to rank importer match confidence
  • Similarity-based ranking optimized for real-world trade data characteristics
