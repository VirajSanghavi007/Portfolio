Speech AI · ASR Ongoing

Hermes

Messenger god - carries meaning across gods and mortals, translates one register into another. A speech system's entire job is that same translation: raw acoustic signal into meaning, and diagnosing exactly where the translation breaks.

A research-grade adaptive speech pipeline that goes beyond transcription. It diagnoses WHY ASR fails at the phoneme level, fingerprints acoustic noise, detects model drift, and closes the loop with targeted LoRA fine-tuning,all in a single system.

Python Wav2Vec2 PyTorch AdaLoRA Bark TTS FastAPI React PostgreSQL Docker

Problem

Standard ASR systems return a transcript and stop. They don't tell you which phonemes are causing errors, what the acoustic environment is doing to the signal, or how the model is drifting over time. ADAPT-Synthetix was designed to close all three gaps.

Architecture

Built on Facebook's Wav2Vec2 with a multi-layer diagnostic engine sitting on top. Every transcription runs through CTC confidence scoring, phoneme error rate computation, reference alignment, and noise fingerprinting before entering a confidence-weighted remediation priority queue.

  • 8-feature acoustic noise fingerprinting: spectral centroid, bandwidth, rolloff, ZCR, RMS, MFCC variance, tempo, harmonic ratio. Classifies into clean / traffic / crowd / machinery / indoor.
  • Phoneme-level drift monitoring using CUSUM and MMD. Triggers fine-tuning alert when 5+ phonemes enter high-risk state across a 20-utterance window.
  • Domain vocabulary injection for medical and emergency terms,these errors are escalated in the priority queue regardless of overall CER.
  • AdaLoRA (adaptive rank) fine-tuning with layer freezing and 10% experience replay. Implements HDMoLE LoRA-MoE routing across multiple expert adapters.
  • Dual frontend: vanilla JS terminal UI (mic, waveform, file upload) and a React 18 dashboard with analytics, phoneme explorer, and model hub pages.

Research Papers Implemented

Rumberg et al. Interspeech 2023 (CTC token uncertainty), Ernez et al. PMLR 2023 (conformal prediction priority queue), Zhang et al. ICLR 2023 (AdaLoRA), Mu et al. ICASSP 2025 (HDMoLE), Guo et al. ICML 2017 (ECE calibration).

What I Learned

Phoneme-level granularity reveals failure modes that word-level CER completely masks. The drift detection was the hardest part: CUSUM is sensitive to window size in ways that are not obvious until you see it fail on real-world noisy audio.

Finance · Quant Trading

Fortuna

Goddess of luck, fate, and fortune - capricious, never fully controllable, only measured and hedged against. A trading system doesn't eliminate that uncertainty, it manages exposure to it - the name is the premise, not decoration.

Multi-asset algorithmic trading system covering equities and crypto in one pipeline: feature engineering, a stacked RF/XGBoost/LightGBM ensemble with regime routing, walk-forward backtesting, SHAP/LIME explainability with drift monitoring, and paper execution via Alpaca - surfaced through a FastAPI backend and a 7-page Streamlit dashboard.

Python XGBoost LightGBM scikit-learn Optuna SHAP FastAPI Streamlit Docker

Problem

Most hobby trading-bot projects either overfit a single backtest window or skip evaluation discipline entirely. AlgoTrade was built to avoid both: every model claim has to survive a walk-forward backtest, not a single train/test split, and every prediction is explainable and drift-monitored rather than trusted blindly.

Architecture

Data pipeline pulls equities via yfinance, crypto via ccxt, and macro indicators via FRED, feeding a shared feature engineering layer. Three base models (RandomForest, XGBoost, LightGBM) train per-asset and feed a stacking ensemble; a regime router switches strategy based on detected market regime. Deep/RL models (PPO, SAC) are supported but optional - the pipeline falls back to the classical ensemble gracefully when torch isn't installed.

  • Walk-forward optimization (WFO) backtesting - rolling re-validation instead of one static split, across 20+ equities and crypto pairs.
  • SHAP/LIME attribution plus KS-test drift monitoring comparing live feature distributions against training data, with an append-only JSONL audit log of every decision.
  • Paper execution via a broker simulator with realistic next-open fills and delta-to-target rebalancing, plus live paper trading through Alpaca.
  • FastAPI backend (REST + a /ws/signals websocket) and a 7-page Streamlit dashboard, both wired to real pipeline data - no mocked views.

What I Learned

Walk-forward backtesting catches overfitting a single split hides completely - several early model configs looked strong on one window and fell apart on the next. Explainability and drift monitoring aren't optional extras for a trading system; without them a silently degrading model just keeps trading until the losses show up in the account, not in a metric you're watching.

Failure Modes in Federated Learning for Fraud Detection

Investigating how Byzantine-robust aggregation schemes in Federated Learning silently discard legitimate updates from institutions with non-IID data distributions, producing models that appear accurate but fail for specific participants.

Python Federated Learning Byzantine Robustness Krum FLAME

Problem

Cross-institutional fraud detection systems using Federated Learning rely on robust aggregation schemes (Krum, FLAME) to filter malicious updates. But these filters cannot distinguish between a malicious update and a legitimate update from a bank with a different but valid data distribution. The result: a model that reports high accuracy while silently failing for specific participants.

Why It Matters

A fraud detection model that works for 90% of banks but fails for the remaining 10% creates an exploit window. Fraudsters who identify these blind spots can operate undetected. What makes this dangerous is the reported accuracy - the central server has no mechanism to flag that its metrics conceal local failure.

Example

Three banks train a joint fraud model via FL. Banks A and B are international with similar profiles, but Bank C is regional with different customers. The aggregation filter sees Bank C's updates as outliers, discards them, and the converged model shows high accuracy - but fails to catch fraud patterns specific to Bank C's population.

Research Context

  • Studying FLAME and Krum aggregation schemes and their behaviour under non-IID conditions
  • Li et al. (IEEE 2024): Byzantine-robust schemes fail even without Byzantine clients when data is non-IID
  • FL adoption accelerating due to GDPR, India's DPDP Act - more deployments mean more severe non-IID conditions
  • No standardized pre-deployment verification protocol exists for FL systems to catch this failure class
